Corsten Douglas - the tutor
Corsten Douglas - the tutor

I am a private tutor with interest and experience in teaching spanning 6 years.

My enthusiasm for teaching began when I obtained a Junior Sports Leadership Award when I was 15. This enabled me to lead PE lessons given to year 7 and 8 students at my high school. My interest in physical education and sports sparked my enthusiasm for learning more about bioenergetics and metabolism.

During my MRes and PhD, I was responsible for instructing undergraduate students to perform experiments and I explained the theory behind each experiment so they could pass exams relating to these topics.

I am involved in continuing professional development for teachers, which emphasises reflective teaching practices to improve the quality of the teaching a student receives.

I volunteered at the very well subscribed 2016 Medical Research Council-Mitochondrial Biology Unit stall at the Cambridge Science Festival. Explanations of various aspects of biomedical research relating to mitochondria and structural biology were demonstrated.

My aim in the next year is to continue to inspire young people to enjoy learning by equipping them with the necessary study skills to excel in any subject.

I have been carrying out proteomics and biochemistry research as a PhD student at the MRC-mitochondrial biology unit in Cambridge for the last 4 years. I studied the assembly pathway of human ATP synthase, supervised by Professor Sir John Walker.

I graduated in October 2017 with a PhD in biological sciences (biochemistry) from the University of Cambridge. I graduated with an MRes in Cancer research at Newcastle University in 2012, and prior to that, I obtained a BSc (Hons) in biochemistry at York University in 2010.

I have and always will be keen to convey my enthusiasm for science to young people, as there is a great need to nurture the development of scientific understanding in order to excel at these sometimes difficult subjects.
